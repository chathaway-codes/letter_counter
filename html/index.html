  3. Given that the EFF DES cracker could examine 92,625,000,000 keys/per second (Look here), I would do the following calculations:

    Time (in days) = (key space)/9e10/(60*60*24)

    36500 = (key space)/9e10/(60*60*24)

    key space = ~2^(68)

    Given that speed, it would take 10 years to "crack" (worst case) a key with 2^68 key size. This seems reasonable to me.

  4. I have here a program to generate graphs for plain text and a simple caesar cipher. The graphs indicate the number of occurances (x-axis) of character (y-axis), ordered from greatest to least. If you want to check a different cipher, just encrypt it elsewhere and try pasting it into the plain text box.

    In general, you will see that the two graphs are identical. This indicates to me that it is very vulnerable to cryptoanalysis (for example, the most common letter is e. Therefore, to decrypt the caesar shift, just find the shift amount between the most common encrypted letter and e, then apply that shift all around).
